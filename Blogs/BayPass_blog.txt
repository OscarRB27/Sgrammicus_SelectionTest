#BayPass

#Parameters used to run BayPass

#Parameters All sites

##Primero vamos a obtener la matriz de covarianza

Analysis/BayPass/Program/g_baypass -npop 4 -gfile Data/BayPass/Input_Loci/SN_baypass_all.input -efile Data/BayPass/Input_Vars/Env_All.txt -outprefix Analysis/BayPass/Results/covis -nval 1000 -thin 25 -burnin 500 -npilot 10 -pilotlength 500

###Ahora toca el análisis de asociación usando el modelo AUX (auxiliar model)
##checa que usamos la matriz de salida "covis_mat_omega.out" obtenida en el paso anterior

Analysis/BayPass/Program/g_baypass -npop 4 -gfile Data/BayPass/Input_Loci/SN_baypass_all.input -omegafile Analysis/BayPass/Results/covis_mat_omega.out -efile Data/BayPass/Input_Vars/Env_All.txt -outprefix Analysis/BayPass/Results/SN_covaux  -nval 1000 -thin 25 -burnin 500 -npilot 10 -pilotlength 500 -auxmodel -nthreads 6

###Regresamos a la consola y anlizamos las simulaciones con los mismos settings

Analysis/BayPass/Program/g_baypass -npop 4 -gfile Analysis/BayPass/Results/G.btapods -outprefix Analysis/BayPass/Results/anapod -nval 1000 -thin 25 -burnin 500 -npilot 10 -pilotlength 500 -nthreads 1 -seed 85934 -nthreads 4


20 pilot runs of 1000 iterations, a run length of 50,000 iterations, sampling every 25th iteration, and a burn-in of 5000 iterations.

20 pilot runs of length 1,000, followed by a main run of length 500,000 with a burnin of 5,000 and a thinning interval of 25
